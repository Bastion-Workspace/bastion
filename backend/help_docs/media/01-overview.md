# Media overview

The **Media** page lets you browse and play **music**, **audiobooks**, and **podcasts** from sources you connect (e.g. Navidrome for music, Audiobookshelf for audiobooks and podcasts). You see **albums**, **artists**, **playlists**, **series**, and **authors**; you can play tracks, manage playlists, and control playback from the **status bar**. This page describes how to open Media, configure sources, and use playback. The Media nav entry may be hidden if no media source is configured.

---

## Opening the Media page

- **Navigation** — Click **Media** in the main navigation (or **Music** if your instance uses that label). The Media page opens with tabs for **Music**, **Audiobooks**, and **Podcasts** (or similar). If you do not see Media, go to **Settings > Media** and add at least one source; the nav entry appears once a source is configured.

---

## Configuring sources (Settings > Media)

- **Music** — Add a **Navidrome** server: base URL and credentials. After saving, the system connects and loads your library (albums, artists, playlists). You can **test** the connection to verify.
- **Audiobooks and podcasts** — Add **Audiobookshelf**: server URL and credentials. Once connected, you get **audiobooks** (by author, series) and **podcasts** (shows and episodes). Test the connection if the UI offers it.

You can have both Navidrome and Audiobookshelf configured. Each source appears in the Media page under the corresponding tab. If a source fails (e.g. server down), the tab may show an error until the connection is restored.

---

## Browsing content

- **Music tab** — Browse by **albums**, **artists**, or **playlists**. Select a view (e.g. “Albums”, “Artists”, “Playlists”) from the sidebar or tabs. Click an album or artist to see **tracks**; click a track to play. You can **search** and **sort** (e.g. by name, date). **Playlists** show the track list; you may be able to add or remove tracks depending on the source and UI.
- **Audiobooks tab** — Browse **audiobooks** by author or series. Open a book to see **chapters** or tracks. Play from the list. **Progress** (e.g. position in the book) may be saved and resumed.
- **Podcasts tab** — Browse **podcast shows** and **episodes**. Select a show to see episodes; play an episode. Progress may be saved.

---

## Playback controls

- **In the Media page** — Use **Play**, **Pause**, **Next**, **Previous**, and **progress bar** (seek) in the player area. Volume may be in the player or system-controlled. **Queue** or **up next** may show when supported.
- **Status bar** — When media is playing, the **status bar** at the bottom of the app shows **music controls** (play/pause, track title, maybe skip). You can control playback without staying on the Media page.

---

## Capability and visibility

- **Media nav entry** — Shown only when at least one **media source** is configured (Settings > Media). If you remove all sources, the Media entry may disappear.
- **Permissions** — Access to Media may be restricted by instance policy (e.g. certain users or roles). If you cannot add sources or see Media, check with your administrator.

---

## Summary

- **Media** is in the main nav; the page has tabs for **Music**, **Audiobooks**, and **Podcasts**. Configure **Navidrome** and **Audiobookshelf** in **Settings > Media**.
- Browse **albums**, **artists**, **playlists**, **series**, and **authors**; **play** tracks from the page or the **status bar**. Media entry is hidden when no source is configured.
