# Messaging

**Messaging** is user-to-user chat inside Bastion. You open the **Messaging** drawer from the top bar (e.g. chat or envelope icon), then pick or create a **room**, add **participants**, and send **messages**. Messages support attachments and reactions, and you see **presence** (online/offline) for participants. This page describes the drawer, rooms, and how to use messaging. It is separate from **team feeds** (posts and comments on a team) and from **external connections** (Telegram, Discord, etc.).

---

## Opening the Messaging drawer

- In the **top bar**, click the **Messaging** icon (e.g. chat bubble or mail icon). A **drawer** opens from the right (or as a panel).
- The drawer shows a list of **rooms** on the left and the **current room’s chat** on the right. You can **expand to full screen** for a larger view and collapse back to the drawer.
- Click the icon again or the **Close** button to close the drawer. A **badge** on the icon may show the total **unread** message count across all rooms.

---

## Rooms

- **Room list** — The drawer lists your **rooms** (conversations with one or more other users). Each room shows a name (or “Unnamed Room”), optional last message preview, and last activity time. Click a room to open its chat.
- **Create room** — Use **New room** or the **Add** (plus) button to create a room. You may be asked to give it a name and add participants. The new room appears in the list.
- **Rename room** — Open the room’s **context menu** (e.g. right-click or three dots) and choose **Rename**. Enter the new name and save. The list updates.
- **Delete room** — From the room’s context menu, choose **Delete**. Confirm when prompted. The room and its message history are removed for all participants; this cannot be undone.
- **Mute** — You can **mute** a room so you do not get notifications for new messages. Unmute from the same menu when you want alerts again.

---

## Participants

- **Add participant** — Open the room’s context menu and choose **Add participant** (or equivalent). Select one or more users from the list and add them. They can then see the room and send messages.
- **Participants list** — The chat header or a participants panel may show who is in the room. You can see **presence** (online/offline or away) next to each participant.

---

## Sending messages

- **Message input** — At the bottom of the room’s chat, type in the input field and press **Send** (or Enter) to post a message. Messages appear in order in the conversation.
- **Attachments** — If the UI supports it, use the attachment or paperclip button to attach files to a message. Recipients can view or download them.
- **Reactions** — You may be able to add **reactions** (e.g. emoji) to a message. Reactions appear on the message; others can add or remove their own reactions.
- **Real-time updates** — New messages and presence updates appear in real time (via WebSocket). You do not need to refresh the page.

---

## Presence

- **Presence indicators** — Next to room names or participant names, the UI may show a **presence** status (e.g. green for online, gray for offline). This helps you see who is active.
- **Your status** — Your own status is updated automatically when you use the app. There may be no separate “away” or “do not disturb” control depending on the instance.

---

## Summary

- **Messaging** is opened from the **Messaging** icon in the top bar. The **drawer** shows **rooms** and the **current room’s chat**.
- **Rooms** can be **created**, **renamed**, and **deleted**; you can **add participants** and **mute** a room.
- **Messages** support **attachments** and **reactions**; **presence** shows who is online. Updates are **real-time**.
