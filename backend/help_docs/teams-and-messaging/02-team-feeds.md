# Team feeds

Each team has a **feed** of **posts** and **comments**. You open the team from **Teams** in the nav, then go to the **Feed** tab to read and write posts and reply with comments. This page describes how the feed works and how it relates to team documents.

---

## Opening the feed

- Go to **Teams** and click a team (or accept an invitation and open the team).
- The team page has tabs: **Feed**, **Members**, **Settings**. Select **Feed** to see the team’s posts and comments.

---

## Posts

- **Creating a post** — In the Feed tab, use the composer (e.g. “Write a post” or similar) to add a new post. Enter your text and submit. The post appears at the top of the feed (or in chronological order, depending on the UI).
- **Reading posts** — Posts are listed in the feed. You can scroll to see older posts. Each post shows the author, time, and content. Click or expand a post to see its **comments**.
- **Editing and deleting** — If the instance allows it, you may see **Edit** or **Delete** on your own posts. Use them to update or remove a post. Deletion is typically permanent.

---

## Comments

- **Adding a comment** — Open a post and type in the comment box at the bottom (e.g. “Add a comment”). Submit to post your reply. Comments appear under the post in order.
- **Reading comments** — Comments are shown under each post. You may see author, time, and content. Replies may be nested or flat depending on the UI.
- **Editing and deleting** — Your own comments may have **Edit** or **Delete** if the instance supports it.

---

## Activity and unread counts

- **Unread** — The system may track which posts or comments you have not read. The **Teams** nav entry or team card can show a **badge** with the number of unread items (e.g. new posts or comments). Opening the feed and viewing posts marks them as read.
- **Notifications** — If you have not **muted** the team, you may get in-app or push notifications for new posts or comments (depending on instance and user settings).

---

## How team documents relate to the feed

- **Team documents** are the shared folder for that team in the Document Library (see **Teams overview**). They are separate from the feed: the feed is for short posts and comments; the team folder is for files (Markdown, Org, PDFs, etc.).
- You can reference or link to documents in posts or comments if the UI supports it. The feed does not replace the folder — use the folder for long-lived content and the feed for updates and discussion.

---

## Summary

- The **Feed** tab on a team’s page shows **posts** and **comments**. Create posts from the composer; add comments under each post.
- **Unread** counts and badges may appear on Teams or the team card; **mute** the team to reduce notifications.
- Team **documents** live in the team’s folder in the Document Library; the feed is for discussion and updates.
