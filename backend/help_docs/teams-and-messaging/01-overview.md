# Teams overview

**Teams** let you collaborate with others: you share a **team folder** in the Document Library and a **team feed** for posts and comments. You can create a team, invite members, join via invitation, and mute teams you want to stay in without notifications. This page describes what teams are, how to create and join them, and where team documents and feeds live.

---

## What teams are

A **team** has:

- **Name** and optional **description** and **avatar**.
- **Members** — Users who have accepted an invitation. Each member has a **role** (e.g. admin, member). Admins can manage the team and members; members can use team documents and the feed.
- **Team documents** — A shared folder that appears in the Document Library under the team’s name. All members can see and edit files there (subject to permissions). The team root folder cannot be deleted; you would remove the team instead.
- **Team feed** — A feed of **posts** and **comments** for that team. You open it from the team’s page on the **Feed** tab.

You access teams from **Teams** in the main navigation. A badge may show pending invitations or unread feed count.

---

## Creating a team

- Go to **Teams** and click **Create Team**.
- In the dialog, enter the **team name** and optionally a **description**. Save.
- The new team appears in your list. Open it to add members, post in the feed, and use the team folder. You can configure **Settings** (e.g. name, description) from the team’s **Settings** tab if you are an admin.

---

## Joining via invitation

When someone invites you to a team, the invitation appears in **Teams** under **Pending Invitations**.

- **Accept** — Click **Accept** (or similar) on the invitation card. You become a member and the team appears in your Teams list and in the Document Library sidebar.
- **Reject** — Click **Reject** to decline. The invitation is removed.

After accepting, open the team from the list to see the **Feed**, **Members**, and **Settings** tabs and to use the team folder.

---

## Team members and roles

- **Members** tab — On the team’s page, open **Members** to see who is in the team and their roles. Team **admins** can invite or remove members and change roles; regular members can usually only view the list (depending on instance settings).
- **Roles** — Typical roles are **admin** and **member**. Admins can edit team settings, manage members, and often delete the team. Members can create and edit content in the team folder and post and comment in the feed.

---

## Team documents (shared folder)

Each team has a **root folder** in the Document Library. In the left sidebar, under **Teams**, you see an entry per team (e.g. “Marketing”, “Engineering”). Click it to expand and browse **subfolders** and **files**. You get the same right-click options as in My Documents: **New Folder**, **Upload Files**, **New Markdown**, **New Org-Mode**, **Rename**, **Move**, **Delete** (for non-root items), **Vectorization**, etc. You cannot delete the team root folder; delete the team from the team’s **Settings** tab if you need to remove the team.

---

## Team feeds and muting

- **Feed** — Open the team and go to the **Feed** tab to see **posts** and **comments**. You can create posts and comment. See **Team feeds** for details.
- **Mute** — If you want to stay in the team but reduce noise, you can **mute** the team. Muted teams may not show unread badges or may show them in a subdued way (behavior depends on the UI). Unmute from the team card or settings when you want notifications again.
