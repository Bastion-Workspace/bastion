# Conversations

Each chat session in Bastion is a **conversation** with its own history and context. Conversations are listed in the chat sidebar; you can create new ones, switch between them, rename or pin them, and delete them when you no longer need them. This page describes how to manage conversations and how history and sharing work.

---

## Conversation list

The **chat sidebar** (on the right) shows a list of your conversations. The **current** conversation is the one whose messages you see and where your next message will go. Click a conversation in the list to switch to it; the messages load and you continue in that thread.

- **New conversation** — Use the **New conversation** action (e.g. plus icon or **History** then **New chat**) to start a fresh conversation. It appears in the list and becomes active. You can start typing immediately.
- **Search** — If the sidebar offers a search field over the list, you can filter conversations by title or preview text to find an old thread quickly.

---

## Renaming and pinning

- **Rename** — The current conversation’s **title** is shown in the sidebar header (e.g. “Chat” or a custom name). Use the edit or rename control (e.g. click the title or a pencil icon) to change it. The new name is saved and shown in the list so you can tell conversations apart.
- **Pin** — Pin important conversations so they stay at the top of the list. Use the **Pin** action from the conversation’s menu or the list item. Pinned conversations are not removed when you clear or archive others; unpin when you no longer need quick access.

---

## Deleting conversations

- **Delete one** — Open the menu for that conversation (e.g. three dots or right-click) and choose **Delete**. Confirm if prompted. The conversation and its message history are removed permanently; you cannot undo.
- **Delete all** — Some UIs offer a **Delete all** or **Clear all** option (e.g. from the history or list menu). Use it to remove every conversation. Confirm when asked; this cannot be undone.

---

## Conversation history and continuity

- **History** — Messages in a conversation are stored on the server. When you switch to a conversation, its messages load and the agent uses them as context for the next reply. You can leave the app and return later; the same conversation is still there.
- **Switching** — Changing to another conversation does not delete the previous one; you can switch back and continue. Each conversation keeps its own thread.
- **Export** — You may be able to **export** the current conversation (e.g. as PDF) from the sidebar or conversation menu. Use it to keep a copy before deleting or for sharing outside the app.

---

## Conversation sharing

If your instance supports **conversation sharing**, you can generate a link or invite so others can view (or optionally continue) a conversation. The exact controls depend on your deployment; look for a **Share** or **Invite** action on the conversation or in its menu. Shared links may be read-only or allow replies depending on configuration.

---

## Summary

- Conversations are listed in the chat sidebar; click one to switch, or create a new one to start fresh.
- Rename conversations from the sidebar so you can identify them; pin the ones you use often.
- Delete single conversations or all conversations from the list; deletion is permanent.
- History is stored per conversation; switching conversations does not erase the previous thread. Export is available when you need a copy.
