# Learning and quizzes

Agents can generate **quiz lessons** from conversation topics. When a lesson is offered, a **Lesson Preview** card appears in the chat with a **Start Quiz** button. Starting the quiz opens an overlay that shows **multiple-choice questions** one at a time, with a **timer** and a limit of **three attempts** per question. Your **score** and **progress** are saved. This page describes how lessons appear, how to take a quiz, and how scoring works.

---

## What lessons and quizzes are

- **Lessons** — A lesson is a set of **questions** (and optional content) generated by an agent (e.g. from a conversation or a request like “quiz me on this”). Each question has a **prompt**, **multiple-choice answers**, a **correct answer**, and optionally an **explanation** and a **time limit** (e.g. 30 seconds).
- **Quiz** — Taking the quiz means answering each question in order. You get up to **three attempts** per question; the **timer** counts down. When you answer correctly or exhaust attempts, the **explanation** may be shown and you move to the next question. At the end you see a **completion screen** with score and time; **progress** is recorded.

---

## How lessons appear in chat

- When an agent generates a lesson, the chat message may include a **Lesson Preview** card (or similar). The card shows the lesson title and a **Start Quiz** button (or “Begin quiz”). Click **Start Quiz** to open the **quiz overlay** and begin the first question. You do not need to leave the chat; the overlay appears on top of the page.

---

## Taking a quiz

- **Quiz overlay** — The overlay shows one question at a time. You see the **question text**, **answer options** (e.g. A, B, C, D), a **timer** (e.g. 30 seconds per question), and optionally an **attempts** indicator (e.g. “2 attempts remaining”).
- **Answering** — Click an answer to submit. If **correct**, you get points (see Scoring), the **explanation** may show, and you click **Next** to continue. If **wrong**, you lose an attempt; after **three wrong** answers (or time-out), the correct answer and explanation are shown and you move on with zero points for that question.
- **Timer** — If the **time runs out** before you answer, it counts as a failed attempt. After three time-outs or wrong answers, the question is marked failed and you proceed.
- **Completion** — After the last question, a **completion screen** shows your **total score**, **maximum possible score**, **percentage correct**, and **time taken**. You can close the overlay; **progress** is sent to the server so it can be shown in Settings or activity history if the instance supports it.

---

## Scoring

- **Points per question** — You earn **3 points** for a correct answer on the **first attempt**, **2 points** on the **second attempt**, and **1 point** on the **third attempt**. **0 points** if you never answer correctly (e.g. three wrong answers or time-outs).
- **Total score** — The sum of points for all questions. The **maximum score** is 3 times the number of questions (if every question is correct on the first try). The completion screen shows **score / max** and **percentage**.

---

## Progress and settings

- **Progress** — When you finish a quiz, the result (lesson, score, time) is sent to the backend (e.g. `POST /learning/progress`). Progress may be visible in **Settings** (e.g. Activity History or a Learning section) or used to suggest follow-up lessons. Exact placement depends on the instance.
- **Starting a quiz** — You can only start from a **Lesson Preview** card in chat when an agent has generated a lesson. There is no standalone “quiz library” in the UI unless your instance adds one.

---

## Summary

- **Lessons** are generated by agents and appear as **Lesson Preview** cards in chat with **Start Quiz**. Click to open the **quiz overlay**.
- **Quiz**: one **multiple-choice** question at a time, **timer** (e.g. 30 s), up to **three attempts** per question. **Scoring**: 3 / 2 / 1 points for correct on 1st / 2nd / 3rd try; 0 if all fail. **Completion** screen shows score and time; **progress** is saved.
- Progress may appear in Settings or activity; there is no separate quiz library unless the instance provides one.
